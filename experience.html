Experience

Here's the working experience that I've done and currently doing

My first working experience was in the summer of 2022, where my fellow tennis mentor Daniel Trinh advised that perhaps I should get some working experience at Brymer Lewis Tennis Academy as a tennis instructor. It was from June to August, so I was one of the 20 tennis instructors working the summer camp for kids between ages 6-12. It was located in Irvine, California, just 20 minutes from where I lived. The summer camp was 5 days a week for 3 months, 3 hours a day. Each court had 8-10 kids, and there were 20 courts. 1 instructor per court. I emphasized funadmental groundstroke techniques so that the kids could enjoy sustained rally games, and I fostered a fun environemnt for them to enjoy learning and developing their love for tennis.

Working at Brymer Lewis Tennis Academy was pretty much the only working experience I had before coming here to BYU. Now, as part of the Men's Tennis Team, I feel like this can be treated as a job. I am training, practicing, competing, and travelling, for my scholarship. My tennis pays for my tuition. And there is pressure to perform at your ability, to perform what you are capable of, in order to maintain your scholarship. As student athletes, we put in a lot of hours on and off court for our sport, and most of the time it takes up all of our hours. It is hard work, and it takes a lot of discipline and dedication.
